Q: What is diabetes? A: Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body either does not produce enough insulin or cannot effectively use the insulin it produces.  Q: What are the common symptoms of diabetes? A: Common symptoms include frequent urination, excessive thirst, unexplained weight loss, fatigue, and blurred vision.  Q: How is diabetes treated? A: Treatment includes lifestyle changes like diet and exercise, monitoring blood sugar levels, and medications such as insulin or oral hypoglycemics.  Q: Can diabetes be cured? A: There is no cure for diabetes, but it can be managed effectively with proper care and lifestyle adjustments.  Q: What is type 1 diabetes? A: Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune condition where the body attacks the insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas. It usually appears in children or young adults.  Q: What is type 2 diabetes?
